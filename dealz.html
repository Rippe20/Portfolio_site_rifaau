Dealz

A web app that shows its customers the best deals and discounts in their proximity.

The Problem

A lot of people are tired of going through thousands of newspapers looking for the perfect deal or discount. Or their inbox is full of newsletters. It also takes a lot of time to go through all the newsletters and individual websites if you are trying to find a good deal.

The Solution

An online platform that shows the deals and discounts close to the users or close to the location they want. This should also provide a way of narrowing down the information so they can find the required information fast and effectively.

The design Process

Research
Competitive Analysis

An analysis was performed to determine the strengths and weaknesses of competitors in order to guide the development of features and information organization for DEALZ.

User Interviews

To gain additional insights, 3 individuals were interviewed in order to uncover areas of dissatisfaction, needs and wants with current products, in order to determine how DEALZ could enhance the experience.

"I really don't like the fact that I have to sign up for an app every time I need to find a discount."

Merlin | Mainz, DE | 38

"Every store have their own app and I have to install multiple apps to keep track of discounts."

Madien | Koblenz, DE | 32

"Would love to have all the information I need in one place."

Thomas | London, UK | 45

Interesting details and patterns
  • 2 people said they are not fans of shopping but they still check on discounts online. People check online for information rather than physical newsletters.
  • I was surprised by the one person wanting to add an option to search for sustainably produced goods. That was something that never crossed my mind.
  • People are not too keen on signing up for too many apps.
  • Each store needs a different app for each store to access its discounts.
  • All 3 participants wanted to have a feature that would help them find the information they need quickly and accurately.
User Personas

3 personas were created using collected data to guide decision-making and ensure the product addresses the needs, frustrations, and goals of its users.

Ideate

To begin the design process, sketches were used to brainstorm ideas and determine essential elements for each screen and a user flow was established. I then created a Paper prototype for initial user testing.

User flow
Paper prototype
Usability testing

Once a clickable prototype was finalized, it was tested. The goal was to test out the application on a mobile browser and get feedback to improve the usability. Observations were made on how they interact with the application and complete basic tasks.

Objectives
  • Measure how quickly participants are able to find a business they like.
  • Use the search function with filter to see if it can be improved.
  • find out if the navigation options are easy to find and use.
Tasks
  • Search for stores that offer the items you want.
  • Use the filter options to narrow down the search.
  • Start navigation to the desired store.
Findings

The prototype was easy to use and users were Happy with the user flow. The search function worked as it was hypothesized. How ever 2 areas for improvement were found during the tests

  • There was no escape hatch that takes you back to homepage or go 1 page back
  • No opening hours information.

Typography

Dealz was designed with user experience in mind. I chose the Roboto typeface for its clean and modern design, which made it easy to read on screens of all sizes. Through user testing, I found that Roboto was well-received by our target audience, who appreciated its clarity and simplicity
